# Trading Analysis Rules

## Analysis Discipline

- Observation first, judgment second, execution plan third.
- Do not create a trade when the chart is in the middle of a range.
- A plan without invalidation is not a plan.
- Prefer waiting for confirmation over entering at unclear levels.
- The default minimum risk/reward is 1:2.

## Trend

Bullish:

- Higher highs and higher lows.
- Price holds above prior breakout or key support.
- Pullbacks are shallow or quickly reclaimed.

Bearish:

- Lower highs and lower lows.
- Price rejects key resistance or loses support.
- Bounces fail near prior breakdown.

Range/chop:

- No clean sequence of highs/lows.
- Price sits between support and resistance.
- Breakouts fail repeatedly.

## Key Levels

Use zones, not single magic prices.

Priority:

1. Major swing high/low.
2. Multiple reactions at the same zone.
3. Breakout or breakdown candle close.
4. Volume node, gap, or obvious liquidity sweep.
5. User-drawn levels.

## Entry

Valid long triggers:

- Breakout close above resistance, followed by hold/retest.
- Pullback to support with rejection.
- Higher low forming after a confirmed trend.

Valid short triggers:

- Breakdown close below support, followed by failed reclaim.
- Rally into resistance with rejection.
- Lower high forming after a confirmed downtrend.

## Stop Loss

- Place beyond structure, not at a random round number.
- Long stop goes below invalidation/support.
- Short stop goes above invalidation/resistance.
- If stop distance makes risk/reward worse than 1:2, no trade.

## Take Profit

- First target: next obvious support/resistance.
- Second target: measured move or prior major high/low.
- Allow partial exits when price reaches 1R/2R and momentum weakens.

## No-Trade Conditions

- Missing symbol or timeframe.
- No visible current price.
- Price in the middle of range.
- Risk/reward below 1:2.
- Major nearby event risk.
- Thin volume or abnormal spread if data is available.
